How to Prepare Your Home for a Minnesota Winter

A straightforward checklist for doors, windows, exterior surfaces and indoor safety.

Cold weather preparation is mostly about controlling drafts, moisture and safety risks before temperatures drop.

Doors and windows

  • Replace damaged weatherstripping.
  • Adjust doors that do not latch tightly.
  • Inspect caulk and visible gaps.

Exterior wood

  • Tighten loose deck and stair boards.
  • Repair unstable railings.
  • Address peeling paint or exposed wood when weather allows.

Indoor safety

  • Test smoke and carbon monoxide detectors.
  • Check handrails and stair lighting.
  • Secure loose shelving and wall-mounted items.

Create a winter repair list

Indoor painting, drywall repair, shelving, fixture replacement and furniture assembly are practical winter projects.
