Minnesota Seasonal Home Maintenance Checklist

A practical spring, summer, fall and winter maintenance guide for Central Minnesota properties.

Minnesota weather puts homes through freeze-thaw cycles, snow, rain, summer heat and rapid temperature changes. A seasonal checklist helps small problems get noticed before they become expensive repairs.

Spring

  • Inspect decks, steps, railings and fences for loose boards or movement.
  • Check exterior caulking around windows and doors.
  • Look for drywall stains that may indicate winter moisture.
  • Clean exterior surfaces and inspect gutters from the ground.

Summer

  • Touch up peeling exterior paint.
  • Repair screens, door hardware and weatherstripping.
  • Inspect outdoor wood for splinters, rot or loose fasteners.
  • Complete pressure washing before staining.

Fall

  • Seal drafts around doors and windows.
  • Test smoke and carbon monoxide detectors.
  • Secure railings, gates and exterior fixtures before snow season.
  • Finish indoor repairs before holiday gatherings.

Winter

  • Watch for condensation, ice buildup and new water stains.
  • Keep entrances safe and repair loose interior handrails.
  • Use the indoor season for painting, shelving and fixture updates.
Tip: Keep a running punch list on your phone and group several small tasks into one visit.
